About

I’m an analytics leader specializing in media measurement — TV attribution, lift analysis, anomaly detection, and the reproducible reporting infrastructure that makes those methodologies useful at scale.

I’ve spent the last decade building these frameworks at JuiceMedia, where I lead the Analytics Insights function. My team measures campaigns across pharma, telemarketing, and media verticals. Before that, I built the analytical foundations of my career in financial services at Encore Capital Group.

What I care about is the connection between rigorous measurement and operational discipline. Most analytics teams have one or the other. The teams that have both are the teams whose work survives executive scrutiny — and whose analysts don’t burn out building the same deck every month.

How I think about analytics work

The job is not to produce decks. The job is to answer hard questions defensibly, deliver the answers in formats stakeholders can actually use, and build the team and infrastructure so neither of those things depends on heroics.

The job description, as I understand it.

I optimize for three things, in this order:

Methodological honesty. If the data doesn’t support the claim, the claim doesn’t get made. If the model has assumptions, the assumptions get stated. If we don’t know, we say we don’t know. This is non-negotiable, even when stakeholders want certainty.

Reproducible delivery. Every recurring deliverable should be code-driven. I’ve written a methodology paper on why. The short version: manual report assembly is the largest source of avoidable cost and risk in modern analytics teams.

Team capacity. A senior analyst’s time is the most valuable resource on any analytics team. I structure work so my analysts spend their hours on novel questions, not on reproducing answers we’ve already given.

Skills, organized honestly

The list of tools I’m comfortable with would be long and not particularly useful. Here’s what I’m actually known for:

Causal measurement — TV attribution, lift analysis, synthetic controls, probabilistic attribution. The methodology side of media measurement.

Anomaly detection — Statistical and ML-based approaches for spike detection, applied to web traffic, conversions, and operational metrics.

Reproducible reporting — Quarto, R Markdown, parameterized rendering, gt tables, publication-quality output. The engineering side of analytics work.

Data foundation — SQL, Parquet, partitioning strategy, AWS (S3, Athena), Polars, DuckDB. The storage and query layer that makes everything else fast.

Stakeholder communication — Translating between Bayesian intervals and “yes, this worked” without losing information in either direction.

Team leadership — Managing analysts (3+ direct reports), defining methodology standards, conducting technical reviews, mentoring junior team members through harder problems.
